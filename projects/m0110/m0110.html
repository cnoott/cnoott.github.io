Introduction

With a simple minimalistic design and an easy-to-use interface, the 1984 Macintosh computer was, as Steve Jobs said, "insanely great."

The keyboard itself encompasses said minimal design making it a <60% keyboard. The switches on the keyboard are 'tall cream alps'. Granted, they take their name to heart as the keyfeel is buttery smooth, though, a bit heavy.



How does a handwired keyboard work?

Structure:
The handwired keyboard consists of a matrix made up of diodes (rows)and wire(collumns)connected to either pin on the switch. As seen in the picture to the right, the rows and collumns act as a XY coordinate grid.
Functionality:
Having a matrix with coordinates allows the microcontroller to 'strobe' each collumn scanning each LOW (0) switch for a HIGH input (1). The pull-up resistor identifies the HIGH input resulting in the microcontroller converting the data (using firmware) to send to the computer.

Materials

Materials consisted of:

  • Diodes
  • Single core wire
